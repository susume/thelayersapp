Guide

What is Tarot?

Tarot is a system of 78 cards used for reflection, insight, and guidance. It's not about predicting the future — it's a tool for exploring your thoughts, feelings, and possibilities.

Major & Minor Arcana

The 22 Major Arcana cards represent life's big themes and spiritual lessons (The Fool, The Tower, The Star…). The 56 Minor Arcana cards deal with everyday situations across four suits.

Suits

Wands (♣ Clubs) = passion, creativity, action. Cups (♥ Hearts) = emotions, relationships, intuition. Swords (♠ Spades) = intellect, conflict, truth. Pentacles (♦ Diamonds) = material world, money, health.

Court Cards

Pages/Jacks = youth, messages, learning. Knights = action, movement, pursuit. Queens = maturity, nurturing, mastery. Kings = authority, leadership, responsibility.

Reversed Cards

A reversed card appears upside-down. Its meaning isn't necessarily "bad" — it often indicates blocked energy, internalized qualities, or the shadow side of the card's upright meaning.

Dropped Cards

If a card falls out while you're shuffling, pay attention — many readers believe these "jumper" cards carry a message your subconscious wants you to see. Arcana lets you record this card separately.

Spreads

A spread is the layout pattern for your cards. Each position has a specific meaning (past, present, future, obstacle, advice, etc.). The cards interact with each other through their positions.

AI vs Classic Readings

AI readings use Google Gemini to weave a personalized narrative. Classic readings use pre-written card meanings assembled into a structured interpretation — no internet required.
