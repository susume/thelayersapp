// What it does

Phone-level protection.
Parent-level control.

Eight tools that protect your child's Android phone — from the network layer to the screen they're reading. All managed behind a PIN only you know.

🛡

VPN-based site blocking

Blocks websites at the DNS level using a local VPN — not a browser plugin your child can uninstall. Works across every app and every browser on the phone.

Network-level · All apps covered
⏰

Scheduled blocks

Block certain websites only during school hours or homework time. Allow them on weekends. Set a schedule once and it enforces automatically every day.

Per-site schedules
🌙

Bedtime lockout

Cut all internet access during set hours. No exceptions — every website and every app loses connectivity during the lockout window. Perfect for bedtime.

Full internet cutoff
🔍

Chat monitoring

Uses Android accessibility services to read on-screen text and flag harmful content in messaging apps. Parents review color-coded alert cards — red for urgent, yellow for caution.

Real-time · All messaging apps
🧠

On-device AI classifier

A TensorFlow Lite toxicity model runs entirely on the phone. It classifies harmful content without sending a single byte to the cloud. Your family's data stays private.

TFLite · Zero cloud dependency
🔑

PIN-protected settings

A 4-digit PIN is required on every app launch. Children cannot view, change, or disable any protection. Even if they know the app is installed, they cannot get in.

Parent access only
🔄

Auto-restart after reboot

The VPN protection restarts automatically when the phone reboots. No need to re-open the app — protection is always on, even after updates or battery drain restarts.

Boot receiver · Always-on

// Questions

Frequently asked.

Layers Guard Mobile creates a local VPN on the device that intercepts DNS requests. When your child tries to visit a blocked site, the request is filtered before it ever leaves the phone. No data is routed through external servers — the VPN runs entirely on-device.
The accessibility service reads text that appears on screen — such as messages in chat apps — and runs it through the on-device AI classifier. If harmful content is detected, the app creates a color-coded alert card for parents to review. The service never sends any text off the device.
All settings are locked behind a 4-digit PIN that only you know. The VPN automatically restarts after a device reboot. While a technically savvy child could force-stop the app from Android settings, the boot receiver will restart protection on the next reboot.
Layers Guard Mobile is currently Android only. iOS does not allow third-party VPN-based DNS filtering or accessibility services in the same way. For Apple devices, consider using the Layers Guard Chrome extension on their computer.
There is no catch. Layers Guard Mobile is free, with no ads, no in-app purchases, and no subscription. It was built by a father who needed this tool for his own children. There is no business model — just a parent sharing a solution with other parents.
The app interface is available in English, Japanese, Chinese, and Vietnamese. You can switch languages at any time from the settings screen.
